\section{Behavior Cloning and AWR}

\begin{frame}{Recall: Behavior Cloning Is the Offline Baseline}
  \Large
  \[
    \mathcal L_{\mathrm{BC}}(\theta)
    =
    -\mathbb E_{(s,a)\sim \dataset}
    \left[\log \pi_\theta(a \mid s)\right]
  \]

  \vspace{0.4cm}
  \normalsize
  Learn to imitate the actions in the offline dataset.

  \vspace{0.3cm}
  It is stable and useful, but it does not use rewards or long-term outcomes.

  \vspace{0.3cm}
  Offline RL starts from BC and asks how to improve it without leaving the data support.
  \note{This is a recall slide, not the first BC introduction. We already discussed BC and its pitfalls. Now frame it as the baseline that AWR, IQL, and IDQL improve on.}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Advantage-Weighted Regression}
  \Large
  \[
    \mathcal L_{\mathrm{AWR}}(\theta)
    =
    -\mathbb E_{(s,a)\sim \dataset}
    \left[w(s,a)\log \pi_\theta(a \mid s)\right]
  \]

  \[
    w(s,a) \propto \exp\left(\frac{A(s,a)}{\lambda}\right),
    \qquad
    A(s,a)=Q(s,a)-V(s)
  \]
  \note{Present AWR as a minimal conceptual step: still supervised learning, but weighted by long-term value. Cite Peng et al. verbally here.}
\end{frame}

  \vspace{0.3cm}
  High-advantage actions are cloned more strongly; low-advantage actions are downweighted.
  \note{Use this example to make AWR intuitive. If the logs contain mixed-quality decisions, AWR preferentially imitates the better parts.}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{AWR Is the First RL Step}
  \Large
  AWR is the smallest conceptual step from imitation learning to reinforcement learning.

  \vspace{0.5cm}
  \normalsize
  It still looks like supervised learning, but the labels are now weighted by long-term value.

  \vspace{0.4cm}
  If logs contain good and bad decisions, AWR gives a way to imitate the better ones more.
  \note{Transition to IQL: AWR needs advantages. IQL is a modern way to learn them in offline data without out-of-distribution maximization.}
\end{frame}
